Restaurants in Manhattan


Thursday Kitchen

Thursday Kitchen can be described as a fusion between Spanish food and Korean food. The owner chef, Kyungmin Kay Hyun created her own concept of "New Korean". With her South Korean background, French cuisine training and love for spanish food, Thursday Kitchen offers a mix between all three cultures in a tapas style fashion. I have tried their eel tacos, steak, edamame dumplings, truffle mac and cheese, kimchi paella and popcorn ice cream. My personal favorites would have to be the edamame dumplings, steak and truffle mac and cheese. Of course, we can't leave out the popcorn ice cream. Though the portions are small, I think it was definitely something to try out.

Thursday Kitchen is located at 424 E 9th St, New York, NY 10009. Here is the link to Google Maps if you would like to go and try it out yourself!

Harbs

Harbs is a very popular cafe in Japan, that has opened several locations in New York City. They are known for their freshness and hand-made cakes. Though on the pricier side, I think it is worth a try, considering how their lunch special is a very good deal! You get a drink, salad, either sandwich, salad or pasta and a cake at the end. I always end up very full when I eat at Harbs. The service is also very good and the overall ambiance is great.

There are three different locations of Harbs in NYC. Chelsea. Broadway. Upper East Side.

Pepper Lunch

Pepper Lunch is another restaurant that originated in Japan, but opened locations in New York City. The idea of this idea is a DIY teppanyaki. You can customize the amount of things you want on your sizzling hot plate. They are known for the ability to cook the meat very quickly on the iron plate. Some facts about the iron plate is that it will heat up to 500 degrees Fahrenheit in 70 seconds and remain hot at around 175 degrees Fahrenheit for more than 20 minutes.

There are two different locations of Pepper Lunch in New York City. Broadway. Chelsea.
